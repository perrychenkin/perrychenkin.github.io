Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset looks at whether or not patient's show up to their appointments, and factors that could impact that. It is taken from the medical information of 100k appointments made in Brazil.

Columns

PatientId - An ID number for each patient \ AppointmentId - An ID number for each appointment\ Gender - Gender of patient\ ScheduledDay - Date and time the appointment was made\ AppointmentDay - Date of appointment\ Age - Age of patient\ Neighbourhood - Town of hospital\ Scholarship - Whether or not patient is enrolled in Bolsa Familia\ Hipertension - Whether or not patient has hypertension\ Diabetes - Whether or not patient is diabetic\ Alcoholism - Whether or not patient is an alcoholic\ Handcap - Whther or not patient is handicap\ SMS_received - If the patient received an SMS message about the appointment\ No-show - If the patient is a no show, 'No' means the patient showed up to the appointment\

Question(s) for Analysis¶

Question 1 \ How frequently do patients not show up?

Question 2 \ Which factors correlate most to a patient not showing up?

Question 3\ What does the average no show patient look like?

Data Cleaning¶

The data is already fairly clean. The PatientId column is presented in e notation, which can be fixed by formatting the column. The ScheduledDay and AppointmentDay columns include both date and time, so they should be split to seperate columns for better analysis, as I will not require the times. The dates will then need to be converted to the proper date format. The No-Show column should be changed from categorical to numeric by using a lambda function to convert Yes to 1 and No to 0. When using describe on the dataframe, I noticed potential outilers in the Age column, as well as ages under 0. I will check for and remove outliers.

Conclusions¶

Through my analysis, I found that roughly 20% of scheduled appointments resulted in the patient not showing up. When looking at how each factor correlates to a patient no show, I found that Date_diff and SMS_received had by far the highest correlation to whether or not a patient showed up. Despite that, one limitation is that the correlation was not statisically signifcant enough to come to any conclusions. More data, such as interviewing patients about why the missed their appointment, is needed to continue this research.
